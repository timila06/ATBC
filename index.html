About us

The leading bilateral business organisation for Australia and Thailand.

The Australian-Thai Business Council (ATBC) is the leading organisation for access, insight, and advocacy in Australian-Thai business relations. The ATBC works to strengthen connections between the Australian and Thai private sectors, fostering mutually beneficial partnerships and promoting business opportunities between the two countries.

It also provides a valuable platform for business leaders and representatives from both nations to connect, exchange ideas, and collaborate on initiatives that support bilateral trade and investment.

Council role

Access, insight, and advocacy for bilateral business.

Established in 1980, ATBC promotes commercial cooperation, facilitates business connections, and advocates on behalf of its members. With representatives based in Sydney, Perth, Adelaide, Brisbane, and Bangkok, the Council provides access to influential government and industry leaders, market intelligence, and high-value networking opportunities.

Monthly Statement

As of July 2026, M.L. Laksasubha Kridakon will assume the role of President of the Australia Thailand Business Council (ATBC). M.L. Laksasubha brings extensive leadership experience, having served as President of the Australian Alumni Association (Thailand) for the past 10 years and as a Board Director of the Australian-Thai Chamber of Commerce (AustCham Thailand) for more than 16 years. She also made history as the first Thai female President of AustCham Thailand. In addition to her leadership roles, she is a successful business owner with interests spanning the hospitality and hotel sector, international education, training and consultancy services, and real estate development. Under her leadership, ATBC is expected to further strengthen economic, trade, and investment ties between Australia and Thailand. Working closely with key organisations such as the Thai Board of Investment (BOI), the Board of Trade of Thailand, the Thai Chamber of Commerce, and various government agencies, she will actively promote bilateral trade, investment, and business development opportunities between the two countries.

Mission & Vision

Vision

A leading non-profit strengthening Australia-Thailand collaboration.

My vision is for the ATBC to be a leading non-profit organisation strengthening trade, investment, and collaboration between Thailand and Australia. Through trade missions, exhibitions, roadshows, and networking events, we will create opportunities for Thai businesses to expand into the Australian market and grow bilateral trade.

We will also promote educational and cultural exchange through student programs, internships, scholarships, and study opportunities, fostering stronger people-to-people connections and long-term cooperation. Through these efforts, ATBC will help build a stronger and more mutually beneficial Australia-Thailand relationship.

Mission

Create practical opportunities across business, trade, and education.

Our mission is to deliver tangible outcomes for members through three core pillars: business networking and engagement, trade missions and roadshows, and education exchange programs. Each pillar is designed to create lasting value for Australian and Thai businesses, institutions, and communities.

Through these activities, ATBC will foster stronger people-to-people connections, long-term commercial cooperation, and a thriving bilateral business environment across both countries.

Business Networking and Engagement

Organise networking events that connect business leaders and stakeholders from Thailand and Australia, while building relationships with potential partner organisations. Our networking program provides a trusted environment for bilateral connections, introductions, and community building across both markets.
